01 — About

Engineering at the intersection
of biology & technology

I am a current Master's student in Mechanical Engineering at UC Berkeley, aiming to combine medicine and technology to improve patient outcomes. My family's extensive professional history in medicine taught me how transformative medicine can be. As an engineer, I aspire to transform medicine in a different way: through technology.

To accomplish that goal, I've accumulated cross-functional industry experience in R&D, quality, and manufacturing. From building automated pressure testers at Stryker to redesigning an entire braid shaping process at Terumo Neuro, I have experience leading projects through full design cycles — from early ideation to verification and validation.

My role developing a mitral valve replacement device at Sutra Medical gave me invaluable experience with design modeling and data-driven decision-making. Now, as I lead my capstone team toward building a fully functional humanoid hand prototype, I continue to refine my technical, personal, and leadership skills.
